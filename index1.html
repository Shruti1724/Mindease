Understanding Mental Health

Learn about common conditions and when to seek professional help

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders involve more than temporary worry or fear. The anxiety doesn't go away and can get worse over time, affecting daily activities.

Common symptoms: Excessive worry, restlessness, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, irritability, muscle tension, and sleep problems.

When to seek help: When anxiety interferes with daily activities and relationships.

Depression

Depression is more than just feeling sad. It's a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest that can affect how you think, feel, and handle daily activities.

Common symptoms: Persistent sadness, loss of interest in activities once enjoyed, changes in appetite, trouble sleeping, fatigue, difficulty concentrating.

When to seek help: If symptoms last for more than two weeks and interfere with your daily life.

PTSD

Post-traumatic stress disorder can develop after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event.

Common symptoms: Flashbacks, nightmares, severe anxiety, uncontrollable thoughts about the event, and avoidance behaviors.

When to seek help: If symptoms persist for months after the traumatic event.

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder causes unusual shifts in mood, energy, activity levels, and the ability to carry out day-to-day tasks.

Common symptoms: Manic episodes (feeling extremely "up," very active, less need for sleep) and depressive episodes (feeling very "down," low energy).

When to seek help: If you experience extreme mood swings that interfere with your life.

OCD

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder is characterized by unwanted, intrusive thoughts and repetitive behaviors performed to reduce anxiety.

Common symptoms: Intrusive thoughts, compulsive behaviors, excessive cleaning, checking, counting, or arranging items.

When to seek help: When obsessions and compulsions take up significant time and interfere with daily life.

Eating Disorders

Eating disorders involve extreme emotions, attitudes, and behaviors surrounding weight and food.

Common symptoms: Preoccupation with weight, body shape, and food, restrictive eating, binge eating, purging behaviors.

When to seek help: If you have persistent thoughts about food, weight, or body image that affect your eating habits.
